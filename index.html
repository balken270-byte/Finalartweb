AGI RUSH

Four labs. One finish line. Be the first to train a superintelligence — or the last lab standing.

THE ECONOMY — WHAT LABS ACTUALLY FIGHT OVER

  • Funding flows in passively from investors. Its rate scales with Trust (public perception) and post-launch hype. Spend it on people and buildings.
  • Compute (FLOPs) is generated by datacenters. Build more to afford bigger training runs.
  • Data is scraped by researchers from the glowing data clusters on the map — a contested, slowly-regenerating commons. From Gen 3, researchers at your HQ also distill synthetic data.
  • Talent is your units. Researchers gather data or accelerate training at HQ. Every researcher working at HQ speeds an active run by 15%.
  • Government Favor is earned by a Lobbyist standing at the Capitol (map center). At 60 Favor you can launch a Regulatory Probe to freeze a rival's training for 20s — and Favor is one of the two keys to deployment.

THE RACE

  • Train 5 model generations — Chatbot → Reasoner → Agent → Innovator → AGI. Each costs compute + data and takes time.
  • Every launch generates hype (more funding) but risks a public incident (−15 Trust).
  • After AGI, the Superintelligence Run unlocks. It needs a mountain of compute and data plus a license to deploy: Trust ≥ 40 or Favor ≥ 60. First to finish it wins. Instantly.
  • Buy Safety Initiatives to restore Trust. Or ignore safety and lobby your way to a license — your call.

CONFLICT

  • Cyber Ops Agents raid rival infrastructure. Burning a leader's datacenters slows their next run. Turrets defend yours.
  • Losing your HQ shuts your lab down permanently. Rivals will raid whoever leads the race — including you.
